# Introduction to AI speech concepts

## Quick summary

This module explains speech recognition, synthesis, feature extraction, and conversational voice experiences.

## Key takeaways

- Speech recognition converts speech to text.
- Speech synthesis converts text to natural speech.
- Feature extraction (for example MFCC-like representations) supports recognition.
- Prosody improves naturalness in synthesized speech.
- Voice systems should support accessibility needs.

## Predictive interview Q&A

### Q1. What is speech-to-text used for?

**Answer:** Captions, command interfaces, call transcription, and voice analytics.

### Q2. Why is prosody important?

**Answer:** It adds natural rhythm, stress, and intonation, improving comprehension.

### Q3. What affects speech recognition quality?

**Answer:** Microphone quality, noise level, accent variation, and model configuration.

### Q4. How does text-to-speech fit accessibility?

**Answer:** It enables audio output for users who prefer or require spoken interfaces.

### Q5. What is speech-to-speech?

**Answer:** A pipeline combining STT, reasoning/translation, and TTS in one conversational flow.
