# Introduction to natural language processing concepts

## Quick summary

This module covers tokenization, statistical text methods, semantic embeddings, and common NLP scenarios.

## Key takeaways

- Tokenization breaks text into machine-processable units.
- TF-IDF helps rank term importance in document collections.
- Embeddings represent semantics in vector space.
- Entity extraction and sentiment analysis are frequent NLP workloads.
- NLP models can produce both structured extraction and open-ended generation.

## Predictive interview Q&A

### Q1. Why is tokenization needed?

**Answer:** Models operate on tokens, not raw paragraphs; tokenization creates those units.

### Q2. When is TF-IDF useful?

**Answer:** When you need keyword importance per document within a larger corpus.

### Q3. What are embeddings used for?

**Answer:** Similarity search, semantic retrieval, clustering, and context-aware reasoning.

### Q4. How does sentiment differ from entity extraction?

**Answer:** Sentiment scores opinion polarity; entity extraction identifies typed items such as names and dates.

### Q5. Why might structured NLP APIs be preferred over free-form generation?

**Answer:** They provide deterministic schema-based outputs for operational workflows.
