# Introduction to computer vision concepts

## Quick summary

This module covers image classification, object detection, segmentation, CNNs, vision transformers, multimodal models, and image generation.

## Key takeaways

- Classification assigns labels to whole images.
- Detection locates objects with regions (for example bounding boxes).
- Segmentation classifies pixels for precise boundaries.
- CNNs and ViTs are major vision model families.
- Multimodal models connect visual and language representations.

## Predictive interview Q&A

### Q1. How does object detection differ from classification?

**Answer:** Detection identifies what and where; classification identifies what overall.

### Q2. When is segmentation preferred?

**Answer:** When precise boundaries or pixel-level masks are required.

### Q3. Why are multimodal models important?

**Answer:** They enable tasks like image captioning and text-image reasoning.

### Q4. What is a common image generation approach?

**Answer:** Diffusion-style iterative denoising conditioned on prompts.

### Q5. Why test in playground before coding?

**Answer:** To validate model behavior and prompt strategy quickly.
