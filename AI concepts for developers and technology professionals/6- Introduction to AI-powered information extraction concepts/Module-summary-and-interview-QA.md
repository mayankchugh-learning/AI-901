# Introduction to AI-powered information extraction concepts

## Quick summary

This module focuses on extracting structured data from documents, audio, and video using schema-based analysis.

## Key takeaways

- Basic OCR extracts text; content understanding maps to meaning and schema.
- Schemas define what fields to extract and how results are structured.
- Analyzers apply extraction logic consistently across inputs.
- Results are typically JSON and suited for automation pipelines.
- Extraction can span documents, calls, and meetings.

## Predictive interview Q&A

### Q1. What is the main value of schema-driven extraction?

**Answer:** Consistent, machine-consumable structured output aligned to business fields.

### Q2. Why use analyzers?

**Answer:** To reuse extraction logic and enforce predictable output structure.

### Q3. What is a nested field example?

**Answer:** Invoice line items containing description, quantity, unit price, and totals.

### Q4. Why is JSON output useful?

**Answer:** It is easy to parse and integrate with search, storage, and workflows.

### Q5. How does this exceed OCR?

**Answer:** It captures relationships and semantic meaning, not only plain text.
